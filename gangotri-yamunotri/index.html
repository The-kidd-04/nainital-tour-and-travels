About Gangotri & Yamunotri

Gangotri and Yamunotri are two of the four sacred shrines that form the revered Char Dham pilgrimage circuit in the Garhwal Himalayas. These ancient temples mark the spiritual origins of India's two holiest rivers -- the Ganga and the Yamuna.

Yamunotri is the source of the River Yamuna and sits at an altitude of 3,293 meters. The temple is reached via a scenic 6 km trek from Janki Chatti, passing through lush forests and hot springs where pilgrims cook rice and potatoes in the naturally boiling water as prasad. The Divya Shila, a rock pillar of immense religious significance, stands near the temple.

Gangotri is the origin of the River Ganga (known as Bhagirathi at its source) at 3,100 meters. The Gangotri Temple, built by Gorkha General Amar Singh Thapa in the 18th century, is a stunning white granite shrine set against towering Himalayan peaks. For the adventurous, the Gaumukh Glacier trek (18 km from Gangotri) takes you to the actual snout of the glacier where the Bhagirathi emerges. The surrounding scenery of snow-clad peaks, dense deodar forests, and roaring rivers is breathtaking.

Gangotri & Yamunotri Itinerary (6 Days)

Day 1

Haridwar to Barkot

Early morning pickup in our Tata Winger. Drive through the scenic Mussoorie-Barkot route via Dehradun. Pass through beautiful pine and deodar forests. Arrive at Barkot by evening. Overnight stay at Barkot.

Distance: ~210 km | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 2

Yamunotri Trek & Darshan

Early drive from Barkot to Janki Chatti (45 km). Begin the 6 km trek to Yamunotri Temple (or take pony/palki). Visit the hot springs, Divya Shila, and the sacred temple. Evening trek back to Janki Chatti. Return to Barkot for overnight stay.

Trek: 6 km each way | Time: 5-6 hrs round trip
Day 3

Barkot to Uttarkashi

After breakfast, drive from Barkot to Uttarkashi. Visit the famous Vishwanath Temple and Shakti Temple in Uttarkashi. Explore the local market. Overnight stay at Uttarkashi.

Distance: ~100 km | Drive: 3-4 hrs
Day 4

Uttarkashi to Gangotri, Darshan

Morning drive to Gangotri through spectacular Himalayan scenery along the Bhagirathi river. Visit the sacred Gangotri Temple, Surya Kund, and Bhagirath Shila. Evening aarti at the temple. Return to Uttarkashi or stay near Gangotri.

Distance: ~100 km | Drive: 3-4 hrs
Day 5

Gangotri Area & Harsil Valley

Explore the stunning Harsil Valley, one of the most beautiful valleys in India. Visit the apple orchards, the serene village of Dharali, and enjoy the panoramic views of snow-capped peaks. A peaceful day amidst pristine Himalayan beauty. Overnight at Uttarkashi.

Harsil is ~25 km before Gangotri
Day 6

Return to Haridwar

Comfortable drive back to Haridwar in the Tata Winger. Optional stop at Tehri Dam viewpoint. Drop at Haridwar by evening.

Distance: ~250 km | Drive: 8-9 hrs

Best Time to Visit Gangotri & Yamunotri

Both Gangotri and Yamunotri temples open in late April/early May (on Akshaya Tritiya) and close in November (on Diwali/Bhai Dooj). The best months to visit are:

  • May - June: Pleasant weather, clear mountain views, ideal for trekking. Temperature: 5°C to 18°C.
  • September - October: Post-monsoon, lush green valleys, fewer crowds, crisp air. Temperature: 2°C to 15°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and landslide risks on the mountain roads.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes -- jacket, thermals, gloves, woolen cap (even in summer)
  • Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Rain poncho / umbrella
  • Personal medicines, first aid
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Power bank for phone

Important Tips

  • Yamunotri trek is 6 km -- moderate difficulty, ponies and palkis available at Janki Chatti
  • Gaumukh glacier trek (18 km from Gangotri) requires a permit from the Forest Department
  • Start treks early (5-6 AM) to avoid afternoon weather changes
  • Try the natural hot spring-cooked rice at Yamunotri -- a unique experience
  • ATMs are scarce beyond Uttarkashi -- carry enough cash
  • Mobile network is limited -- BSNL works in some areas
  • Both temples are part of Char Dham -- can be combined with Kedarnath and Badrinath
