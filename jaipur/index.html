About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is famously known as the "Pink City" due to its stunning terracotta-pink buildings. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, this royal city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most colorful destinations in India.

From the iconic honeycomb facade of Hawa Mahal to the magnificent Amber Fort perched on a hilltop, from the astronomical wonders of Jantar Mantar to the panoramic views from Nahargarh Fort — Jaipur is a treasure trove of history, culture, and Rajasthani hospitality. The vibrant bazaars, delicious cuisine, and warm people make every visit memorable.

Jaipur Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Arrival & City Sightseeing

Arrive Jaipur by morning. Visit Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), City Palace, and Jantar Mantar observatory. Afternoon visit to Albert Hall Museum. Evening shopping at Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar for jewelry, textiles, and handicrafts.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 2

Amber Fort, Nahargarh & Jal Mahal

Morning visit to Amber Fort — enjoy the elephant/jeep ride up, see Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace). Drive to Nahargarh Fort for panoramic city views. Stop at Jal Mahal (Water Palace) for photos. Evening visit Birla Mandir at sunset.

Distance: ~30 km round trip | Full day
Day 3

Jaigarh Fort & Departure (or Ajmer/Pushkar Extension)

Morning visit to Jaigarh Fort to see the world's largest cannon (Jaivana). Last-minute shopping for block-print fabrics and blue pottery. Depart for Delhi or extend trip to Ajmer Sharif / Pushkar.

Departure or extension | Half day

Best Time to Visit Jaipur

  • October - March: Winter season — perfect weather (8°C - 25°C). Best time for sightseeing. Jaipur Literature Festival in January.
  • April - June: Extremely hot (35°C - 45°C). Avoid unless you love the heat. Cheaper hotel rates.
  • July - September: Monsoon brings relief from heat. Green Aravallis, dramatic skies. Occasional heavy rain.

Things to Carry

  • Light cotton clothes — Rajasthan is hot most of the year
  • Comfortable walking shoes (lots of fort climbing!)
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat — the sun is intense
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated at all times
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Cash — many local shops and bazaars prefer cash
  • Camera — every corner of Jaipur is photogenic!

Important Tips

  • Book a composite ticket at the first monument — it covers Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, City Palace, Jantar Mantar, and Nahargarh
  • Visit Amber Fort early morning to avoid crowds and heat
  • Negotiate prices firmly at bazaars — start at 40-50% of quoted price
  • Try authentic Rajasthani thali — Dal Baati Churma, Ghevar, Laal Maas
  • Watch the sunset from Nahargarh Fort — one of the best views in India
  • All major networks (Jio, Airtel, Vi) work well throughout Jaipur city
