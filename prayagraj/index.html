About Prayagraj

Prayagraj, formerly known as Allahabad, is one of the holiest cities in India and is home to the sacred Triveni Sangam — the confluence of three rivers: Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati. It is believed that a holy dip at the Sangam washes away all sins and breaks the cycle of rebirth.

Prayagraj is world-famous for the Kumbh Mela, the largest religious gathering on Earth, held every 12 years. The city also holds immense historical significance — it was a centre of the Indian freedom movement, home to the Nehru family's Anand Bhawan, and the place where the Indian National Congress was founded. The Allahabad Fort built by Emperor Akbar, the towering Allahabad University, and the beautiful Civil Lines area add charm to this ancient city.

Prayagraj Trip Itinerary (1-2 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Prayagraj — Arrival & Sangam Darshan

Early morning departure from Delhi. Travel via Agra-Lucknow Expressway and reach Prayagraj by evening. Head straight to Triveni Sangam for a holy boat ride to the confluence point. Take a sacred dip where the three rivers meet. Visit Hanuman Mandir and Alopi Devi Mandir near the Sangam area. Evening at leisure.

Distance: ~630 km | 9-10 hrs drive
Day 2

Sightseeing & Return Journey

Morning visit to Allahabad Fort (exterior view — inside is army area) and the Akshay Vat (immortal banyan tree) and Patalpuri Temple inside the fort. Visit Anand Bhawan and Swaraj Bhawan (Nehru family homes, now museums). See the Allahabad Pillar and Khusro Bagh. After lunch, begin the return journey.

Local sightseeing + return journey

Best Time to Visit Prayagraj

  • October - March: Winter season — pleasant weather (8°C - 25°C). Best time for Sangam visit. Magh Mela in January-February is a grand spiritual experience.
  • April - June: Extremely hot (38°C - 47°C). Avoid unless necessary. The heat in Prayagraj is intense.
  • July - September: Monsoon brings relief but rivers swell significantly. Sangam boat rides may be restricted during heavy rains.

Things to Carry

  • Extra set of clothes — you will need dry clothes after the holy dip at Sangam
  • Comfortable and modest clothing — it's a religious destination
  • Slip-on footwear for temple visits
  • Water bottle and snacks — especially during Mela season when stalls get crowded
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Cash — boatmen and local vendors mostly accept cash only
  • Sunscreen and hat — especially in winter afternoons which can still be sunny

Important Tips

  • Negotiate boat fare before boarding at Sangam — fixed rates are usually displayed
  • Visit Sangam early morning for the most peaceful and spiritual experience
  • During Magh Mela / Kumbh Mela, book accommodation well in advance
  • Allahabad Fort requires permission for full interior visit — plan accordingly
  • Try local specialities — Prayagraj ki chaat, kachori, and Loknath ki lassi
  • All major networks (Jio, Airtel, Vi) work well throughout Prayagraj city
