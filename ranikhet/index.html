About Ranikhet

Ranikhet is a quiet and charming cantonment town maintained by the Indian Army's Kumaon Regiment. Unlike the bustling tourist spots, Ranikhet offers genuine peace — clean roads, manicured gardens, and pristine pine and oak forests stretching as far as the eye can see. It is significantly less crowded than Nainital, making it perfect for those seeking tranquility.

The town is home to the Upat Golf Course, one of the highest golf courses in Asia, offering stunning Himalayan views. Jhula Devi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Durga, is famous for its thousands of brass bells hung by devotees. Chaubatia Gardens is a sprawling fruit orchard with apple, plum, and peach trees, set against a backdrop of magnificent Himalayan peaks.

Best Time to Visit Ranikhet

Ranikhet is pleasant for most of the year. The best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Comfortable weather, blooming orchards, clear mountain views. Temperature: 10°C to 25°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon freshness, lush green forests, excellent visibility. Temperature: 8°C to 20°C.

Avoid July-August due to monsoon rains. Winter (Dec-Feb) is cold but peaceful, with occasional snowfall.

Things to Carry

  • Light woolens in summer, heavy woolens in winter
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Camera for the scenic views
  • Light rain jacket during monsoon months
  • Personal medicines
  • Power bank for phone

Important Tips

  • Ranikhet is a cantonment area — maintain cleanliness and follow rules
  • The KRC Museum may have restricted timings — check beforehand
  • Carry cash as ATM availability is limited in some areas
  • Ranikhet is much less crowded than Nainital — perfect for a relaxed holiday
  • Mobile network works well for most operators
  • Try the local Kumaoni cuisine at small eateries
