About Kausani

Mahatma Gandhi called Kausani the "Switzerland of India" — and for good reason. This serene hill station offers an unparalleled 300km panoramic view of the Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Panchachuli. The breathtaking sunrise and sunset views over these snow-capped mountains are nothing short of legendary.

Kausani is home to the famous Uttarakhand Tea Estate, where you can walk through lush tea gardens and taste fresh Kumaon tea. The town also offers Rudradhari Falls, a beautiful waterfall surrounded by dense forest, and the ancient Baijnath Temple — a remarkable Shiva temple dating back to the 12th century. Gandhi himself stayed at the Anasakti Ashram here, where he wrote his commentary on the Bhagavad Gita, inspired by the tranquil surroundings.

Kausani Trip Itinerary (3 Days)

Day 1

Drive from Nainital / Almora to Kausani

Scenic drive through pine forests and mountain roads to Kausani. Check in at your hotel and head out for the legendary evening sunset view over the Himalayan peaks. Watch as Nanda Devi and Trishul turn golden in the setting sun.

Distance: ~120 km from Nainital | ~55 km from Almora
Day 2

Tea Gardens, Rudradhari Falls & Baijnath Temple

Morning visit to the Uttarakhand Tea Estate — walk through the tea gardens and taste fresh local tea. Head to Rudradhari Falls for a refreshing experience in nature. Afternoon visit to the ancient Baijnath Temple, a stunning 12th-century Shiva temple with intricate carvings.

All attractions within 15-20 km of Kausani
Day 3

Sunrise View, Anasakti Ashram & Return

Wake up early for the magical sunrise over the Himalayas — the 300km panorama at dawn is unforgettable. Visit Anasakti Ashram where Mahatma Gandhi stayed and wrote. After breakfast, begin the return journey with beautiful memories.

Return: ~120 km to Nainital | ~55 km to Almora

Best Time to Visit Kausani

Kausani is beautiful year-round, but the best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, clear Himalayan views, blooming flowers. Temperature: 10°C to 25°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon clarity, stunning views, comfortable weather. Temperature: 5°C to 20°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains. Winter (Dec-Feb) brings cold but offers snow-capped views on clear days.

Things to Carry

  • Light woolens in summer, heavy woolens in winter
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Camera — the views demand it
  • Light rain jacket (especially in monsoon months)
  • Personal medicines
  • Power bank for phone

Important Tips

  • Wake up early for sunrise — it's the highlight of any Kausani trip
  • Carry cash — ATMs are limited in Kausani
  • The tea estate is open on weekdays — check timings before visiting
  • Baijnath Temple is about 17 km from Kausani — plan your time accordingly
  • Mobile network works well for Jio and Airtel
  • Book accommodation in advance during peak season (May-June)
