About Rishikesh

Rishikesh — where spirituality meets adventure. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas on the banks of the holy Ganga, Rishikesh is a unique destination that offers both inner peace and adrenaline-pumping thrills. Known worldwide as the Yoga Capital of the World, this sacred town attracts spiritual seekers, adventure junkies, and culture lovers alike.

The town is home to iconic landmarks like Lakshman Jhula, Ram Jhula, Triveni Ghat, the Beatles Ashram (Maharishi Mahesh Yogi Ashram), and the ancient Neelkanth Mahadev Temple. It is also one of India's top adventure destinations — famous for river rafting (Grade 3-4 rapids), bungee jumping (India's highest at 83 meters), cliff jumping, and riverside camping. The mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat every evening is an unmissable experience. Rishikesh is often combined with Haridwar, just 20 km away, where the grand Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri is equally spectacular.

Rishikesh Trip Itinerary (3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Rishikesh — Evening Ganga Aarti

Early morning pickup from Delhi in our Tata Winger. Scenic drive to Rishikesh via NH58 (approx 5-6 hours). Check in to your hotel or campsite. In the evening, head to Triveni Ghat for the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti ceremony. Stroll along the ghats, soak in the spiritual vibes.

Distance: ~240 km | Drive: 5-6 hrs
Day 2

River Rafting & Sightseeing

Morning river rafting session — choose 16 km or 26 km stretch with Grade 3-4 rapids. After rafting, explore Lakshman Jhula, Ram Jhula, and the famous Beatles Ashram. Spend the afternoon cafe hopping at the many riverside cafes. Evening free for yoga sessions or relaxation.

Rafting: 16 km or 26 km | Sightseeing: Half day
Day 3

Neelkanth Mahadev, Haridwar & Return

Morning visit to Neelkanth Mahadev Temple (hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Shiva). Then drive to Haridwar (20 km). Visit Har Ki Pauri for the grand Ganga Aarti. After aarti, begin the return drive to Delhi. Drop-off by late night.

Rishikesh → Haridwar: 20 km | Haridwar → Delhi: ~220 km

Best Time to Visit Rishikesh

Rishikesh can be visited almost year-round, but the best months are September to May. For adventure activities like rafting, the ideal windows are:

  • September - November: Post-monsoon, the river is thrilling with strong rapids. Lush green surroundings. Great weather.
  • February - May: Pleasant weather, perfect for rafting, camping, and sightseeing. Temperature: 15°C to 35°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains. River rafting is usually suspended during this period for safety.

Things to Carry

  • Change of clothes (you will get wet during rafting)
  • Waterproof phone pouch
  • Comfortable shoes / sandals with grip
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Light jacket (evenings can be cool, especially Oct-Feb)
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Cash (some adventure operators don't accept cards)
