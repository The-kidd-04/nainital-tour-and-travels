About Badrinath

Badrinath is one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Situated in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand at an altitude of 3,133 meters, the Badrinath Temple is one of the 108 Divya Desams and holds immense spiritual significance for Hindus across the world.

The temple is believed to have been established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century. Set against the majestic backdrop of the Neelkanth Peak (6,597m), Badrinath offers a deeply spiritual and visually stunning experience. The sacred Tapt Kund hot spring near the temple is believed to have healing properties. Nearby Mana Village, the last Indian village before the Tibet border, and the spectacular Vasudhara Falls add to the charm of this divine destination.

Badrinath Trip Itinerary (4 Days)

Day 1

Haridwar to Joshimath

Early morning pickup in our Tata Winger. Drive through scenic route via Devprayag, Rudraprayag, Karnaprayag, and Chamoli to Joshimath (approx 10-11 hours). Enjoy stunning views of river valleys and mountain passes. Overnight stay at Joshimath.

Distance: ~275 km | Drive: 10-11 hrs
Day 2

Joshimath to Badrinath — Evening Darshan

Morning drive from Joshimath to Badrinath (approx 1.5 hours). Check in to your accommodation. Take a holy dip in the Tapt Kund hot spring. Evening darshan at the Badrinath Temple. Witness the mesmerizing Aarti ceremony. Overnight stay near the temple.

Distance: ~45 km | Drive: 1.5 hrs
Day 3

Mana Village, Vasudhara Falls & Tapt Kund

Early morning darshan at the temple. Visit Mana Village — the last Indian village before the Tibet border. Explore Vyas Gufa (cave where Ved Vyas composed the Mahabharata), Ganesh Gufa, and Bhim Pul. Trek to the stunning Vasudhara Falls (5 km from Mana). Return and relax at Tapt Kund. Overnight stay.

Mana Village: 3 km | Vasudhara Falls: 5 km trek
Day 4

Return Journey

Comfortable drive back in the Tata Winger. Optional stops at Joshimath, Devprayag (Sangam of Alaknanda & Bhagirathi rivers), or Rudraprayag. Drop at Haridwar/Rishikesh by evening.

Distance: ~320 km | Drive: 10-12 hrs

Best Time to Visit Badrinath

Badrinath Temple opens in May (on Akshaya Tritiya) and closes in November (on Bhai Dooj). The best months to visit are:

  • May - June: Pleasant weather, clear skies, ideal for darshan and sightseeing. Temperature: 7°C to 18°C.
  • September - October: Post-monsoon, clear mountain views, fewer crowds. Temperature: 4°C to 15°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and landslide risks on the roads.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — jacket, thermals, gloves, woolen cap (even in summer)
  • Comfortable walking shoes with good grip
  • Rain poncho / umbrella
  • Personal medicines, first aid
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Power bank for phone

Important Tips

  • Badrinath Temple opens early morning (4:30 AM) — reach early to avoid long queues
  • Take a dip in Tapt Kund before entering the temple (it is customary)
  • Mana Village is a must-visit — plan at least half a day for it
  • Vasudhara Falls trek is moderate (5 km) — carry water and snacks
  • ATMs are limited in Badrinath — carry enough cash
  • Mobile network is limited — BSNL works in some areas
