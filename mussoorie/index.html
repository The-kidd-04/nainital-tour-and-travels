About Mussoorie

Mussoorie is a charming hill station nestled in the foothills of the Garhwal Himalayan range, just 35 km from Dehradun. Often called the "Queen of Hills," this picturesque town has been a favourite retreat since the British era, offering stunning views of the Shivalik ranges and the Doon Valley.

From the bustling Mall Road to the thundering Kempty Falls, from the panoramic views at Gun Hill to the serene Cloud's End — Mussoorie has something for everyone. Lal Tibba, the highest point, offers breathtaking Himalayan views, while Company Garden is perfect for a relaxed family outing. It's one of the most popular honeymoon and family destinations in North India.

Visit the stunning Kempty Falls (15 km from Mussoorie). Head to Gun Hill via cable car for panoramic Himalayan views. Explore Company Garden with its beautiful flower beds and mini lake. Evening walk along the scenic Camel's Back Road.

Morning visit to Lal Tibba — the highest point in Mussoorie with stunning views of Himalayan peaks. Drive to Cloud's End, the last accessible point of Mussoorie, surrounded by thick oak and deodar forests. Begin comfortable return journey to Delhi/Dehradun.

Distance: ~290 km to Delhi | ~35 km to Dehradun

Best Time to Visit Mussoorie

Mussoorie is a year-round destination, but the best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, ideal for families and honeymooners. Temperature: 15°C to 30°C. Perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon clear skies, lush green hills, stunning Himalayan views. Temperature: 10°C to 20°C. Fewer crowds.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and potential landslides on hill roads.

Things to Carry

  • Light woolens — sweater, light jacket (even in summer, evenings are cool)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for Mall Road and sightseeing
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Camera to capture the stunning views
  • Personal medicines, if any
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
