About Char Dham Yatra

The Char Dham Yatra is the most sacred pilgrimage circuit in Uttarakhand, encompassing four divine shrines nestled in the mighty Himalayas. These four dhams — Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath — together form a spiritual journey that every devout Hindu aspires to complete at least once in their lifetime.

Yamunotri is the source of the River Yamuna and the seat of Goddess Yamuna. Gangotri marks the origin of the holy River Ganga and is dedicated to Goddess Ganga. Kedarnath, at an altitude of 3,583 meters, houses one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Badrinath, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, sits between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges and is one of the 108 Divya Desams.

The great Adi Shankaracharya established this pilgrimage circuit in the 8th century to revive Hinduism across India. He is credited with founding the temples at Kedarnath and Badrinath, and the circuit has since been a cornerstone of spiritual life in India. Completing the Char Dham Yatra is believed to wash away all sins and pave the path to salvation (moksha).

Char Dham Yatra Itinerary (10 Days)

Day 1

Haridwar to Barkot (Yamunotri Base)

Early morning pickup from Haridwar in our Tata Winger. Drive through the scenic Mussoorie-Barkot highway passing through Dehradun and Mussoorie. Reach Barkot by evening. Overnight stay at Barkot.

Distance: ~210 km | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 2

Yamunotri Temple Visit (6 km Trek)

Drive from Barkot to Janki Chatti (the last motorable point). Begin the 6 km trek to Yamunotri Temple. Take darshan at the temple, visit the Surya Kund (hot water spring) and Divya Shila. Trek back and return to Barkot. Overnight stay.

Trek: 6 km each way | Time: 5-6 hrs round trip
Day 3

Barkot to Uttarkashi

After breakfast, drive from Barkot to Uttarkashi. Visit the famous Vishwanath Temple and Shakti Temple in Uttarkashi. Explore the town and rest. Overnight stay at Uttarkashi.

Distance: ~100 km | Drive: 3-4 hrs
Day 4

Uttarkashi to Gangotri Temple Visit

Early morning drive to Gangotri. Visit the sacred Gangotri Temple dedicated to Goddess Ganga. Take darshan and witness the beautiful confluence point. Drive back to Uttarkashi. Overnight stay.

Distance: ~100 km one way | Drive: 3-4 hrs each way
Day 5

Uttarkashi to Guptkashi

Long scenic drive from Uttarkashi to Guptkashi via Tehri and Srinagar. Pass through beautiful Garhwal valleys. Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple at Guptkashi. Overnight stay.

Distance: ~220 km | Drive: 8-9 hrs
Day 6

Guptkashi to Kedarnath (Trek 16 km)

Early drive to Gaurikund (last motorable point). Begin the 16 km trek to Kedarnath Temple (or take pony/palki/helicopter). Reach Kedarnath by afternoon. Evening darshan at the temple. Overnight stay near the temple.

Trek: 16 km | Time: 6-8 hrs trekking
Day 7

Kedarnath Back to Guptkashi

Early morning darshan at Kedarnath Temple. Trek back down to Gaurikund (4-5 hours). Our Tata Winger will be waiting. Drive back to Guptkashi. Rest and overnight stay.

Trek: 16 km down | Drive: 1 hr
Day 8

Guptkashi to Badrinath via Joshimath

Drive from Guptkashi to Badrinath via Chamoli and Joshimath. En route, enjoy stunning views of the Alaknanda valley. Reach Badrinath by evening. Visit Tapt Kund (hot spring). Overnight stay at Badrinath.

Distance: ~210 km | Drive: 8-9 hrs
Day 9

Badrinath Darshan & Mana Village Visit

Early morning darshan at Badrinath Temple (dedicated to Lord Vishnu). Visit Mana Village — the last Indian village before the Tibet border. See Vyas Gufa, Ganesh Gufa, and the Saraswati River. Explore Bheem Pul. Evening aarti at the temple. Overnight stay.

Mana Village: 3 km from Badrinath
Day 10

Badrinath to Haridwar (Return)

Early morning departure from Badrinath. Comfortable drive back to Haridwar in the Tata Winger via Joshimath, Rudraprayag, and Devprayag. Optional stop at Devprayag to witness the Sangam of Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers. Reach Haridwar by late evening. Yatra complete.

Distance: ~320 km | Drive: 10-11 hrs

Best Time to Visit Char Dham

The Char Dham temples open in late April/early May (on Akshaya Tritiya) and close in November (on Bhai Dooj). The best months to undertake the yatra are:

  • May - June: Pleasant weather across all four dhams, clear skies, ideal conditions for trekking to Yamunotri and Kedarnath. Roads are in good condition.
  • September - October: Post-monsoon freshness, lush green valleys, fewer crowds at the temples. Slightly colder but very beautiful.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains, landslide risks, and road closures across the circuit.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — heavy jacket, thermals, gloves, woolen cap, muffler (essential for 10+ days in the mountains)
  • Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip (for Yamunotri and Kedarnath treks)
  • Rain poncho / umbrella / rain gear
  • Personal medicines, first aid kit, and any prescribed medication
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID) — mandatory for yatra registration
  • Sufficient cash — ATMs are scarce or non-functional in remote areas
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Power bank for phone
