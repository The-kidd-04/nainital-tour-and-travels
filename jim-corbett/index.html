About Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park is India's first national park, established in 1936 as Hailey National Park. Named after the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, it is one of the most famous wildlife destinations in the world and the flagship project of Project Tiger in India.

The park is renowned for its Bengal tigers, wild elephants, leopards, and over 600 species of birds. Spread across dense sal forests, riverine belts, and grasslands, Corbett offers an unforgettable wildlife experience. The park has 5 safari zones — Bijrani, Jhirna, Dhikala, Durga Devi, and Sitabani — each with its own unique landscape and wildlife. Dhikala zone is the most popular and requires advance booking. Jeep safaris and canter safaris are both available. Don't miss the Corbett Waterfall and the Jim Corbett Museum at Kaladhungi. Night stays inside the park at forest rest houses offer a truly immersive jungle experience.

Jim Corbett Trip Itinerary (3 Days)

Day 1

Drive to Corbett, Corbett Waterfall & Evening at Resort

Pickup and drive to Jim Corbett National Park. Visit the scenic Corbett Waterfall en route. Check in at resort and spend a relaxing evening surrounded by jungle.

Day 2

Wake up early for a morning jeep safari (6 AM departure). Spot tigers, elephants, deer, and exotic birds. Return for lunch and rest. Head out again for the afternoon safari for more wildlife sightings.

Day 3

Morning Safari, Kaladhungi Museum & Return

Final morning safari session. After breakfast, visit the Jim Corbett Museum at Kaladhungi to learn about the legendary conservationist. Drive back to your starting point.

Kaladhungi: ~30 km from Corbett

Best Time to Visit Jim Corbett

The park is open year-round, but different zones have different opening periods:

  • November - June: Best overall period. Most zones open. Pleasant weather for safaris.
  • Jhirna & Sitabani zones: Open year-round.
  • Dhikala zone: Open November to June. Book well in advance.

The park core zones close during monsoon (July-September). Jhirna and Sitabani remain accessible.

Things to Carry

  • Binoculars and camera with zoom lens
  • Earth-toned clothing (avoid bright colors in safari)
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Light jacket for early morning safaris
  • Valid ID proof (required for safari permits)
  • Water bottle and light snacks

Important Tips

  • Safari permits must be booked in advance online, especially for Dhikala zone
  • Jeep safari accommodates 6 people; canter safari accommodates 16 people
  • Maintain silence during safari for best wildlife sightings
  • Carry cash — limited ATM availability inside the park area
  • Follow all park rules — no honking, no littering, no getting off the vehicle
  • Forest rest house stays inside the park require prior booking through the forest department
