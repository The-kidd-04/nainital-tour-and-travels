About Naukuchiatal

Naukuchiatal, meaning "the lake of nine corners," is the deepest lake in Uttarakhand. Located just 26 km from Nainital, it offers a peaceful and serene escape without the crowds that Nainital is known for.

The lake is surrounded by dense oak forests, making it a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Activities like paragliding, kayaking, and paddle boating are available at the lake. The calm and quiet atmosphere makes it an ideal weekend escape from city life. You can combine Naukuchiatal with Bhimtal and Sattal for a complete lakes circuit — a perfect 2-day trip exploring the lesser-known but equally beautiful lakes of the Kumaon region.

Naukuchiatal Trip Itinerary

Option 1

Day Trip from Nainital (26 km)

Morning drive from Nainital to Naukuchiatal (26 km, about 1 hour). Spend the day kayaking, paddle boating, birdwatching, or simply relaxing by the lake. Try paragliding if available. Return to Nainital by evening.

Distance: ~26 km | Drive: 1 hr
Option 2

2-Day Lakes Circuit (Bhimtal + Sattal + Naukuchiatal)

Day 1: Drive to Bhimtal, explore the lake island and aquarium. Continue to Sattal for birdwatching and nature walks. Overnight stay near Sattal or Naukuchiatal. Day 2: Morning at Naukuchiatal — kayaking, paragliding, lake walk. Return by afternoon.

Total circuit: ~50 km between lakes

Best Time to Visit Naukuchiatal

Naukuchiatal can be visited throughout the year:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, ideal for outdoor activities and boating. Temperature: 15°C to 30°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon, clear skies, lush greenery, fewer tourists.
  • December - February: Cold but beautiful. Misty mornings over the lake.

Monsoon (July-August) brings rain but the greenery is at its peak. Roads remain accessible.

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light jacket or sweater (evenings can be cool)
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Binoculars for birdwatching
  • Camera for the scenic lake views
  • Water bottle and snacks

Important Tips

  • Naukuchiatal is much quieter than Nainital — perfect if you want peace
  • Paragliding availability depends on weather and season
  • Carry cash — limited ATM/card options at the lake
  • Combine with Bhimtal and Sattal for the best lakes experience
  • Local legend says no one has seen all nine corners of the lake at once
  • Best time for birdwatching is early morning
