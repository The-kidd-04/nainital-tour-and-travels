About Nainital

Nainital — the beautiful lake city shaped like an eye (Naina = eyes) — is one of the most popular hill stations in Uttarakhand. Nestled in the Kumaon foothills of the outer Himalayas, this charming town is built around the emerald-green Naini Lake, which is surrounded by seven majestic hills.

The town is home to iconic attractions like the Naina Devi Temple, Snow View Point, Tiffin Top, Mall Road, the Nainital Zoo, Cave Garden, and Eco Cave. Whether you want a peaceful boating experience on the lake, a cable car ride to the snow viewpoint, or a leisurely stroll on Mall Road — Nainital has something for everyone. It is one of the most popular family destinations in North India.

Nainital Tour Itinerary (3 Days)

Day 1

Pickup & Drive to Nainital

Pickup from Delhi or any city in our Tata Winger. Scenic drive to Nainital through the Kumaon hills. Arrive by evening. Enjoy boating at Naini Lake as the sun sets. Evening stroll on the famous Mall Road — shop, eat, and soak in the hill town vibe.

Distance: ~300 km from Delhi | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 2

Nainital Sightseeing

Full day exploring Nainital. Cable car ride to Snow View Point for panoramic Himalayan views. Trek to Tiffin Top (Dorothy's Seat). Visit the sacred Naina Devi Temple by the lake. Explore Eco Cave Garden and the Nainital Zoo (Pt. G.B. Pant High Altitude Zoo).

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Nearby Lakes & Return

Morning visit to the stunning nearby lakes — Bhimtal, Sattal, and Naukuchiatal (the lake of nine corners). Each lake has its own unique charm. After lunch, begin the return journey. Drop-off at your city by evening.

Nearby lakes: ~20-30 km | Return drive: 7-8 hrs

Best Time to Visit Nainital

Nainital is a year-round destination, but the best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, perfect for sightseeing and boating. Temperature: 10°C to 27°C. Peak tourist season.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon freshness, clear skies, fewer crowds. Temperature: 7°C to 22°C. Great for photography.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and occasional landslides on the hill roads.

  • Camera — the views are stunning
  • Binoculars — for Snow View Point and bird watching at Sattal
