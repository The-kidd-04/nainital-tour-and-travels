About Shitlakhet

Shitlakhet is a tiny hamlet in the Almora district of Uttarakhand, ideal for those seeking complete relaxation and a break from the noise of everyday life. This is not a place for busy sightseeing — it is a place for being still.

Surrounded by dense pine and oak forests, Shitlakhet offers breathtaking 180-degree Himalayan views that will leave you speechless. There are no commercial crowds here — just birdsong, mountain air, and silence. The night sky is perfect for stargazing, with minimal light pollution. The Syahi Devi Temple trek is a gentle walk through the forest. Birdwatching enthusiasts will find this place a paradise. Located just 28 km from Ranikhet, Shitlakhet is perfect for couples, writers, artists, and nature lovers who want nothing but peace and quiet.

Shitlakhet Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Drive from Nainital/Almora, Nature Walks & Evening Views

Morning drive to Shitlakhet from Nainital or Almora. Check in at your homestay or resort. Spend the afternoon on gentle nature walks through pine forests. Watch the sunset paint the Himalayan peaks in golden hues.

Nainital: ~60 km | Almora: ~30 km
Day 2

Syahi Devi Temple Trek & Return via Ranikhet

Early morning birdwatching. Trek to Syahi Devi Temple through the forest — a peaceful walk with beautiful views. After the trek, drive back via Ranikhet for a quick visit to the charming cantonment town.

Ranikhet: ~28 km from Shitlakhet

Best Time to Visit Shitlakhet

Shitlakhet is beautiful in every season:

  • March - June: Perfect weather for nature walks and temple treks. Temperature: 10°C to 25°C.
  • September - November: Clear skies, stunning Himalayan views, ideal for photography.
  • December - February: Cold and misty, occasional snowfall. Best for those who love winter solitude.

Monsoon (July-August) brings lush greenery but roads can be slippery.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — evenings and mornings are cold even in summer
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Binoculars for birdwatching
  • Books, journal, or sketchpad — perfect place for creative pursuits
  • Torch for evening walks and stargazing
  • Cash — no ATMs in Shitlakhet
  • Power bank — charging points may be limited

Important Tips

  • Mobile network is very limited — embrace the digital detox
  • No restaurants or shops — your accommodation will provide meals
  • Book homestays or KMVN guest house in advance
  • Best stargazing happens on new moon nights
  • Carry all essentials as the nearest market is Ranikhet (28 km)
  • This is a place for silence and slow living — plan accordingly
