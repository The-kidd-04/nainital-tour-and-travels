About Lansdowne

Lansdowne is one of the quietest hill stations in India. Maintained by the Indian Army — specifically the Garhwal Rifles regiment — the town is exceptionally clean, well-organized, and free from the chaos that plagues most tourist hill stations.

The Tip-N-Top viewpoint offers sweeping views of the Shivalik ranges. Bhulla Lake is a charming artificial lake where you can enjoy paddle boating. St. Mary's Church stands as a relic from the British era. The War Memorial pays tribute to the brave soldiers of the Garhwal Rifles. For nature lovers, the trek to Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple through dense forest is a must-do. Bhim Pakora, a fascinating balancing rock, defies gravity. Lansdowne is a designated no-honking zone, adding to its peaceful character.

Lansdowne Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Drive from Delhi/Haridwar, Bhulla Lake, Tip-N-Top & War Memorial

Morning drive to Lansdowne. After check-in, visit Bhulla Lake for a peaceful boat ride. Walk up to Tip-N-Top viewpoint for panoramic mountain views. Visit the Garhwal Rifles War Memorial and Regimental Museum to learn about the regiment's history.

Delhi: ~270 km | Haridwar: ~95 km
Day 2

Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple Trek & Return

Morning trek to Tarkeshwar Mahadev Temple through dense oak and deodar forests — a serene and spiritual experience. Visit Bhim Pakora (balancing rock) and St. Mary's Church. Drive back to Delhi or Haridwar by evening.

Tarkeshwar: ~38 km from Lansdowne

Best Time to Visit Lansdowne

Lansdowne is pleasant throughout the year:

  • March - June: Best weather for sightseeing and trekking. Temperature: 12°C to 28°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon freshness, clear views, fewer tourists.
  • December - February: Cold with occasional snowfall. Cozy and romantic. Temperature: 2°C to 12°C.

Monsoon (July-August) brings heavy rain but the lush greenery is stunning. Roads remain accessible.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — Lansdowne gets cold in evenings and winter
  • Comfortable walking shoes for the Tarkeshwar trek
  • Camera — the views are beautiful
  • Light rain jacket or umbrella
  • Water bottle and snacks
  • Valid ID proof
  • Cash — limited ATM availability

Important Tips

  • Lansdowne is a no-honking zone — enjoy the silence
  • Being a cantonment area, some areas may have restricted access
  • The town shuts down early — plan activities during daytime
  • Book accommodation in advance during weekends and holidays
  • Tarkeshwar Mahadev trek is moderate — suitable for most fitness levels
  • Respect the army cantonment rules and keep the town clean
