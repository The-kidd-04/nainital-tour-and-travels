About Khatu Shyam Ji

Khatu Shyam Ji Temple is one of the most revered Hindu temples in India, located in the town of Khatu in Sikar district of Rajasthan. The temple is dedicated to Barbarik, the grandson of Bhima (Pandava), who is worshipped as Shyam Baba.

According to legend, Lord Krishna was so impressed by Barbarik's devotion and sacrifice that he blessed him saying, "In Kaliyug, you will be worshipped as Shyam and whoever takes your name with true devotion will have their wishes fulfilled." The temple attracts millions of devotees every year, especially during the Phalguna Mela and on every Ekadashi and Dwadashi.

Khatu Shyam Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Khatu Shyam Ji

Early morning departure from Delhi in our Tata Winger. Drive via NH48 (Jaipur Highway) and then to Sikar. Reach Khatu by afternoon. Evening aarti and darshan at the temple. Overnight stay at Khatu.

Distance: ~320 km | Drive: 6-7 hrs
Day 2

Khatu Shyam Darshan & Nearby Temples

Early morning Mangla Aarti (4:30 AM). Full darshan at the temple. Visit nearby Khatushyamji Kund, Shyam Bagicha. Optional visit to Salasar Balaji Temple (60 km) or Jeenmata Temple. Return to Khatu for evening aarti.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Return to Delhi (or Jaipur sightseeing)

Morning darshan and prasad. Drive back to Delhi. Optional stop at Jaipur for sightseeing (Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort). Reach Delhi by evening.

Distance: ~320 km | Drive: 6-7 hrs

Best Time to Visit Khatu Shyam

  • October - March: Best time — pleasant weather (10°C - 25°C). Comfortable for darshan and travel.
  • February - March: Phalguna Mela — the biggest festival. Lakhs of devotees visit. Book early!
  • Every Ekadashi & Dwadashi: Special puja and huge crowds — very auspicious days.

Avoid April-June if possible — Rajasthan gets extremely hot (40°C+).

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable clothes suitable for temple visit
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses (Rajasthan sun is strong)
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated
  • Cash for temple donations and prasad shops
  • Valid ID proof
  • Warm clothes if visiting in winter (Dec-Jan nights are cold)

Important Tips

  • Reach early for Mangla Aarti (4:30 AM) — the most sacred aarti of the day
  • VIP darshan passes are available — check at the temple counter
  • Keep your footwear at designated shoe stands near the temple
  • Photography is not allowed inside the main sanctum
  • Try local Rajasthani food — Dal Baati Churma, Kachori
  • Combine with Salasar Balaji (60 km) for a complete pilgrimage experience
