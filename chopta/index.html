About Chopta & Tungnath

Chopta is a lush green meadow sitting at 2,680 meters, often called "Mini Switzerland" for its stunning beauty. It serves as the base camp for the Tungnath trek — home to the world's highest Shiva temple at 3,680 meters. This is one of the Panch Kedar temples and a far less crowded alternative to Kedarnath, offering an equally powerful spiritual experience.

The 3.5 km trek from Chopta to Tungnath is moderate and suitable for beginners. From Tungnath, a further 1.5 km trek leads to Chandrashila peak, which rewards trekkers with a breathtaking 360-degree panorama of Himalayan giants including Nanda Devi, Trishul, Kedarnath, Chaukhamba, and Bandarpunch. Nearby Deoriatal lake, with its crystal-clear reflections of Chaukhamba peak, is another must-visit. Chopta is also a birding paradise, home to rare Himalayan species.

Chopta & Tungnath Trek Itinerary (3 Days)

Day 1

Drive to Chopta from Rishikesh / Haridwar via Ukhimath

Early morning pickup in our Tata Winger. Drive through the scenic route via Devprayag, Rudraprayag, and Ukhimath to Chopta. Enjoy the changing landscapes — from river valleys to dense forests to open meadows. Arrive at Chopta by evening and soak in the mountain views.

Distance: ~195 km from Rishikesh | ~210 km from Haridwar | Drive: 7-9 hrs
Day 2

Trek to Tungnath Temple & Chandrashila Peak

Early morning start for the 3.5 km trek from Chopta to Tungnath — the world's highest Shiva temple. The trail passes through rhododendron forests and alpine meadows. After darshan at the temple, continue 1.5 km to Chandrashila peak for a stunning 360° Himalayan panorama. Return to Chopta by evening.

Trek: 3.5 km to Tungnath + 1.5 km to Chandrashila | Time: 5-7 hrs round trip
Day 3

Deoriatal Lake Visit & Return

Morning visit to the stunning Deoriatal lake — a crystal-clear lake reflecting the mighty Chaukhamba peaks. Spend time in nature, take photographs, and enjoy the serenity. After lunch, begin the return journey to Rishikesh/Haridwar in our Tata Winger.

Deoriatal: ~25 km from Chopta | Return: ~195 km to Rishikesh

Best Time to Visit Chopta

Chopta offers different experiences across seasons. The best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Rhododendrons in bloom, green meadows, clear Himalayan views. Temperature: 5°C to 20°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon clarity, golden meadows, excellent trekking weather. Temperature: 0°C to 15°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and slippery trails. Winter (Dec-Feb) brings heavy snow — only for experienced snow trekkers.

Things to Carry

  • Warm layers — jacket, thermals, gloves, woolen cap (essential at 3,680m)
  • Sturdy trekking shoes with good grip
  • Rain poncho / waterproof jacket
  • Trekking pole (helpful for the Chandrashila climb)
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Personal medicines, first aid kit
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, lip balm
  • Power bank and torch

Important Tips

  • Start the Tungnath trek early (6-7 AM) to avoid afternoon clouds
  • Carry enough cash — there are no ATMs at Chopta
  • Acclimatize properly — Chopta is at 2,680m and Tungnath at 3,680m
  • Basic food and accommodation available at Chopta — don't expect luxury
  • Mobile network is very limited — BSNL has some coverage
  • This is a great alternative to the crowded Kedarnath trek
