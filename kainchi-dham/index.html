About Kainchi Dham

Kainchi Dham is one of the most famous ashrams in India, established by the revered saint Neem Karoli Baba (also known as Maharaj-ji). Located just 17 km from Nainital on the Nainital-Almora road, this peaceful spiritual retreat sits beautifully between two hills in a serene valley.

The ashram gained worldwide fame after it was revealed that Apple co-founder Steve Jobs visited Kainchi Dham in the 1970s seeking spiritual guidance. Years later, Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg also visited the ashram on Jobs' recommendation. This connection has made Kainchi Dham a destination for spiritual seekers from around the world.

Every year on June 15, a grand bhandara (community feast) is organized at the ashram that attracts lakhs of devotees from across India. The ashram radiates a calm and divine energy that visitors find deeply moving, regardless of their spiritual background.

Best Time to Visit Kainchi Dham

Kainchi Dham can be visited throughout the year, but the best months are:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing. The June 15 bhandara is the biggest annual event.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon clarity, beautiful autumn colours in the valley.

The ashram remains open year-round, but visiting during monsoon (July-August) may be affected by rain and occasional landslides on the road.

Things to Know

  • Photography is not allowed inside the ashram premises
  • Dress modestly — the ashram is a place of worship
  • Mobile phones should be kept on silent inside the ashram
  • Free prasad (food) is served at the ashram
  • Remove shoes before entering temple areas
  • The ashram is usually open from 7 AM to 5 PM (timings may vary)
  • No entry fee — the ashram is open to all

Important Tips

  • Arrive early to avoid crowds, especially on weekends and during the June 15 bhandara
  • Combine your trip with a visit to Nainital Lake, Bhimtal, or Almora
  • Carry light woolens even in summer — the valley can get cool
  • ATMs are not available near the ashram — carry cash for any needs
  • Parking is available near the ashram but fills up quickly on busy days
