About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and the spiritual capital of India. Situated on the banks of the holy river Ganga, this sacred city has been a centre of learning, culture, and devotion for over 3,000 years.

From the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat to the ancient Kashi Vishwanath Temple, from peaceful boat rides at sunrise past 84 ghats to the Buddhist pilgrimage site of Sarnath — Varanasi is a city that touches your soul. The narrow lanes (galis) filled with the aroma of street food, the world-famous Banarasi silk sarees, and the timeless chants along the river make every visit a life-changing experience.

Varanasi Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Varanasi — Arrival

Early morning departure from Delhi. Travel via Lucknow Expressway and reach Varanasi by evening. Check in to hotel, freshen up, and head to Dashashwamedh Ghat for the spectacular evening Ganga Aarti ceremony. Walk through the ancient lanes and enjoy Banarasi street food.

Distance: ~820 km | 12-13 hrs drive
Day 2

Full Day Darshan — Temples, Ghats & Boat Ride

Early morning boat ride on the Ganga at sunrise — witness the ghats coming alive with prayers and rituals. Visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple (Jyotirlinga), Sankat Mochan Temple, Tulsi Manas Mandir, and Durga Temple. Explore the famous ghats — Manikarnika, Assi, and Harishchandra. Evening Ganga Aarti again. Shop for Banarasi silk sarees and local handicrafts.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Sarnath Visit & Return Journey

Morning visit to Sarnath (10 km from Varanasi) — where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon. See the Dhamek Stupa, Ashoka Pillar, Sarnath Museum, and the Thai/Japanese/Chinese temples. After lunch, begin the return journey to Delhi or your next destination.

Sarnath: ~10 km | Return journey

Best Time to Visit Varanasi

  • October - March: Winter season — pleasant weather (8°C - 25°C). Best time for sightseeing and temple visits. Dev Deepawali in November is magical — thousands of diyas on the ghats.
  • April - June: Very hot (35°C - 45°C). Early mornings and evenings are still good for ghat visits. Fewer crowds.
  • July - September: Monsoon season. The Ganga swells and ghats may be partially submerged. Humid but fewer tourists.

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable and modest clothing — Varanasi is a religious city
  • Comfortable walking shoes (lots of walking on ghats and narrow lanes)
  • Slip-on footwear for temple visits
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated at all times
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Cash — many local shops, boatmen, and vendors prefer cash
  • Camera — every ghat and lane is photogenic!

Important Tips

  • Reach Dashashwamedh Ghat by 6:30 PM to get a good spot for the Ganga Aarti
  • Book a sunrise boat ride — it's the most peaceful experience in Varanasi
  • Visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple early morning to avoid long queues
  • Try authentic Banarasi food — Kachori Sabzi, Tamatar Chaat, Lassi, Malaiyo (winter special)
  • Bargain firmly when buying Banarasi silk — buy from trusted shops
  • All major networks (Jio, Airtel, Vi) work well throughout Varanasi city
