About Haridwar

Haridwar is one of the oldest living cities in India, a place where ancient spirituality flows as freely as the sacred Ganga herself. The name literally means "Gateway to God," and for millions of pilgrims, it is exactly that — the starting point for the Char Dham Yatra and a destination of deep devotion in its own right.

The iconic Har Ki Pauri ghat marks the exact spot where the Ganga leaves the mountains and enters the plains. The evening Ganga Aarti here is a spectacular experience — hundreds of oil lamps floating on the river, chanting filling the air, and the ghat glowing with divine energy. It is a sight that stays with you forever.

Beyond the ghats, Haridwar offers the Mansa Devi Temple (accessible by cable car), Chandi Devi Temple, Daksha Mahadev Temple, and the peaceful Shantikunj Ashram. The Kumbh Mela, held here every 12 years, draws millions of devotees from around the world. For shopping, Bara Bazaar is the place to find religious items, rudraksha, and souvenirs. And the street food — aloo puri, lassi, and jalebi — is an experience in itself.

Haridwar Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Arrive Haridwar — Har Ki Pauri, Mansa Devi & Ganga Aarti

Arrive in Haridwar and check in. Visit the famous Har Ki Pauri ghat and take a holy dip in the Ganga. Head to Mansa Devi Temple via cable car for panoramic views of the city. In the evening, witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri — the highlight of any Haridwar visit.

Key Stops: Har Ki Pauri, Mansa Devi Cable Car, Evening Ganga Aarti
Day 2

Chandi Devi, Daksha Mahadev & Bara Bazaar

Morning visit to Chandi Devi Temple. Explore the ancient Daksha Mahadev Temple and the serene Shantikunj Ashram. Spend time shopping at Bara Bazaar for religious items and souvenirs. Return journey or continue to Rishikesh for adventure and more exploration.

Key Stops: Chandi Devi Temple, Daksha Mahadev, Shantikunj, Bara Bazaar
