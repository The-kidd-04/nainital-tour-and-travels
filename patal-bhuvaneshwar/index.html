About Patal Bhuvaneshwar

Patal Bhuvaneshwar is an ancient limestone cave temple located about 90 feet underground near the town of Gangolihat in Pithoragarh district of Uttarakhand. This mystical cave is believed to be the abode of 33 crore (330 million) gods and goddesses of the Hindu pantheon.

The cave is entered through a narrow opening, and visitors descend via a series of steep steps into the depths of the earth. Inside, the natural stalactite and stalagmite formations have taken shapes that remarkably resemble various Hindu deities, including Lord Shiva, Lord Ganesha, Sheshnag, Airavat (Lord Indra's elephant), and many more. The formations are believed to be thousands of years old and continue to grow.

Being an off-beat destination, Patal Bhuvaneshwar is not crowded like other popular pilgrimage sites. This makes it a perfect destination for those seeking a unique spiritual experience away from the usual tourist circuits.

Patal Bhuvaneshwar Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Drive to Gangolihat

Early morning departure from Almora or Haldwani in the Tata Winger. Drive through scenic Kumaon hills via Berinag or Didihat to reach Gangolihat. The journey offers stunning views of the Himalayan peaks. Check in to your hotel and rest. Evening visit to the famous Haat Kalika Temple in Gangolihat, the regimental deity of the Kumaon Regiment.

Distance: ~100 km from Almora | ~190 km from Haldwani
Day 2

Cave Temple Visit & Return

Morning drive to Patal Bhuvaneshwar cave (1.5 km from Gangolihat). Descend into the cave with a local guide who explains the significance of each formation. The cave visit takes about 1-1.5 hours. After the visit, begin the return journey to Almora or Haldwani. Our Tata Winger will be ready for a comfortable drive back.

Cave visit: 1-1.5 hrs | Return drive: 4-6 hrs

Best Time to Visit Patal Bhuvaneshwar

The cave temple is open throughout the year. The best months to visit are:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, clear skies, comfortable for the cave descent. Temperature: 15°C to 30°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon, clean roads, beautiful mountain views. Temperature: 10°C to 25°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains. The roads to Gangolihat can be affected by landslides during the monsoon season.

Things to Know

  • The cave entrance is narrow — people with claustrophobia should be cautious
  • Wear comfortable shoes with good grip — the steps inside can be slippery
  • A local guide is mandatory and available at the cave entrance
  • The descent involves steep steps — moderate fitness level required
  • Photography may be restricted inside the cave
  • Carry a light jacket — the cave temperature is cool even in summer

Important Tips

  • Start early to make the most of daylight for the drive
  • Visit Haat Kalika Temple in Gangolihat — it's a must-see nearby
  • Combine with a visit to Jageshwar (ancient Shiva temples) or Almora for a longer trip
  • Carry enough cash — ATM availability is limited in Gangolihat
  • Mobile network coverage is limited — BSNL works best in this area
  • Book accommodation in Gangolihat in advance during peak season
