Char Dham Yatra 2026 — Complete Guide

Everything You Need to Know Before Starting the Sacred Yatra

Updated April 2026 12 min read Uttarakhand

The Char Dham Yatra is the most sacred pilgrimage in Hinduism — a journey to four divine shrines nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. Every Hindu aspires to complete this yatra at least once in their lifetime. This guide covers everything you need to plan your 2026 Char Dham Yatra.

The Four Dhams

YamunotriSource of Yamuna River (3,293m)
GangotriSource of Ganga River (3,100m)
KedarnathLord Shiva's Jyotirlinga (3,583m)
BadrinathLord Vishnu's Abode (3,133m)
Total Duration10-12 Days
Best TimeMay-June & Sep-Oct

Char Dham Yatra Route & Itinerary (10-12 Days)

The traditional route follows a specific order — West to East:

Day 1

Delhi / Haridwar to Barkot

Start from Delhi/Haridwar in our Tata Winger. Drive via Dehradun and Mussoorie to Barkot. Scenic drive through Kempty Falls area. Overnight at Barkot.

Distance: ~210 km from Haridwar | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 2

Barkot to Yamunotri & Back

Drive to Janki Chatti (40 km). Trek 6 km to Yamunotri Temple. Take darshan at the temple and holy hot springs (Surya Kund). Trek back. Overnight at Barkot.

Trek: 6 km each way | Altitude: 3,293m
Day 3

Barkot to Uttarkashi

Drive to Uttarkashi. Visit Vishwanath Temple and Shakti Temple. Rest and prepare for Gangotri. Overnight at Uttarkashi.

Distance: ~100 km | Drive: 4-5 hrs
Day 4

Uttarkashi to Gangotri & Back

Drive to Gangotri Temple (100 km). Darshan at the temple — origin of River Ganga. Visit Surya Kund and Bhagirath Shila. Return to Uttarkashi.

Distance: 100 km each way | Altitude: 3,100m
Day 5

Uttarkashi to Guptkashi

Long but scenic drive via Tehri Dam, New Tehri, and Srinagar Garhwal to Guptkashi. Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple. Overnight at Guptkashi.

Distance: ~220 km | Drive: 8-9 hrs
Day 6

Guptkashi to Kedarnath

Drive to Gaurikund (30 km). Trek 16 km to Kedarnath Temple (or helicopter). Evening darshan at the Jyotirlinga. Overnight near temple.

Trek: 16 km | Altitude: 3,583m
Day 7

Kedarnath to Guptkashi

Early morning darshan. Trek back down to Gaurikund. Our Tata Winger will be waiting. Drive back to Guptkashi. Rest and overnight.

Trek: 16 km down | Drive: 1 hr
Day 8

Guptkashi to Badrinath

Scenic drive via Chopta, Joshimath to Badrinath. Stop at Joshimath for Narsingh Temple. Evening darshan at Badrinath Temple. Overnight at Badrinath.

Distance: ~240 km | Drive: 9-10 hrs
Day 9

Badrinath Darshan & Mana Village

Early morning darshan at Badrinath Temple and Tapt Kund hot spring. Visit Mana Village (last Indian village before Tibet border), Vasudhara Falls, Vyas Gufa, and Bhim Pul.

Local sightseeing | Altitude: 3,133m
Day 10-11

Badrinath to Rudraprayag / Haridwar

Return journey via Devprayag (Sangam of Alaknanda & Bhagirathi). Stop at Rudraprayag for night. Next day drive to Haridwar/Rishikesh. Evening Ganga Aarti.

Distance: ~330 km | Drive: 10-12 hrs total
Day 12

Haridwar to Delhi (Optional)

Morning Ganga snan and darshan. Drive back to Delhi in the Tata Winger. Reach by evening. Yatra complete!

Distance: ~230 km | Drive: 5-6 hrs

Char Dham Yatra 2026 — Registration

Since 2022, biometric registration is mandatory for all Char Dham pilgrims. Here's what you need:

  • Online registration: Register at the official Uttarakhand Tourism portal before starting
  • Biometric at Sonprayag: Mandatory biometric scan at Sonprayag for Kedarnath
  • Medical certificate: Required for pilgrims above 50 years (get from a registered doctor)
  • ID proof: Carry Aadhaar Card / Voter ID (original + photocopy)

Best Time for Char Dham Yatra

  • May - June: Temples open (usually May 1st week). Pleasant weather, clear skies. Most popular months.
  • September - October: Post-monsoon. Lush green valleys, fewer crowds. Second best window.
  • Avoid July - August: Heavy monsoon rains, landslides, road closures. Very risky.

Packing List for Char Dham

  • Warm clothes — thermals, fleece jacket, woolen cap, gloves (even in summer)
  • Rain jacket / poncho (essential)
  • Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Walking stick (buy at Gaurikund if needed)
  • Personal medicines + Diamox (for altitude sickness)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ and sunglasses
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Power bank (charging not available everywhere)
  • Torch/headlamp for early morning treks
  • Aadhaar Card + photocopies (3-4 copies)
  • Cash — ATMs are scarce after Rudraprayag

Important Tips

  • Start early each day — mountain weather changes rapidly after noon
  • Acclimatize properly — spend a day in Uttarkashi or Guptkashi before high-altitude temples
  • Book Kedarnath helicopter well in advance (IRCTC or private operators)
  • Carry enough cash — UPI/cards don't work reliably in most Char Dham areas
  • BSNL has the best coverage in remote areas — get a BSNL SIM
  • Travel in a group of 10-14 in a Tata Winger — most cost-effective option
  • Don't attempt the yatra without an experienced hill driver — the roads demand it
