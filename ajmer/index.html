About Ajmer Sharif

Ajmer Sharif Dargah is the tomb of the revered Sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti, located in Ajmer, Rajasthan. It is one of the most important Muslim pilgrimage sites in India, visited by people of all faiths seeking blessings and spiritual peace.

The Dargah was built by Mughal Emperor Humayun and later expanded by Akbar, Shah Jahan, and other rulers. The complex includes the beautiful Buland Darwaza, Nizam Gate, and the Jama Masjid. Just 14 km from Ajmer lies the holy city of Pushkar, home to the only Brahma Temple in the world and the sacred Pushkar Lake.

Ajmer-Pushkar Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Ajmer

Early morning departure from Delhi. Drive via NH48 (Jaipur Highway) to Ajmer. Arrive by afternoon. Visit Ajmer Sharif Dargah for evening prayers and ziyarat. Explore the bazaar around the Dargah. Overnight stay at Ajmer.

Distance: ~400 km | Drive: 6-7 hrs
Day 2

Ajmer Sightseeing & Pushkar

Morning visit to Dargah for Fajr prayers. Visit Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra, Ana Sagar Lake, Taragarh Fort. Afternoon drive to Pushkar (14 km). Visit Brahma Temple, Pushkar Lake, ghats, and local markets. Return to Ajmer.

Ajmer + Pushkar (14 km) | Full day
Day 3

Return to Delhi

Final darshan at the Dargah. Drive back to Delhi. Optional stop at Jaipur for quick sightseeing (Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort). Reach Delhi by evening.

Distance: ~400 km | Drive: 6-7 hrs

Best Time to Visit Ajmer

  • October - March: Best time — pleasant weather (10°C - 25°C). Comfortable for darshan and sightseeing.
  • Urs Festival: 6-day annual celebration at the Dargah (dates vary by Islamic calendar). Massive gathering of devotees.
  • Pushkar Camel Fair: November — world-famous camel fair with cultural events.

Avoid April-June — Rajasthan heat can be extreme (40°C+).

Things to Carry

  • Modest clothing for Dargah visit (head covering recommended)
  • Comfortable walking shoes (lots of walking at the Dargah)
  • Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses
  • Cash for offerings (chadar, flowers) and local shopping
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated
  • Valid ID proof

Important Tips

  • Remove shoes before entering the Dargah complex
  • Beware of unauthorized "khadims" (guides) — use official Dargah services
  • Best time for darshan: early morning or after evening prayers
  • Pushkar is a holy city — alcohol and non-veg food are not sold here
  • Try Ajmer's famous Sohan Halwa and Kadhi Kachori
  • Combine with Khatu Shyam (170 km) for a complete pilgrimage trip
