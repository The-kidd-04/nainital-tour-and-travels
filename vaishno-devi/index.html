About Vaishno Devi

Vaishno Devi, one of the holiest Hindu pilgrimage sites in India, is a sacred cave temple nestled in the Trikuta Mountains of Jammu & Kashmir. Dedicated to Mata Vaishno Devi (also known as Mata Rani), the shrine receives over 8 million devotees every year, making it the second most visited religious shrine in India.

The holy cave houses three natural rock formations called Pindis, representing the three forms of the goddess — Maha Kali, Maha Lakshmi, and Maha Saraswati. The 12 km trek from Katra to Bhawan (the cave temple) is a deeply spiritual journey through the beautiful Trikuta Mountains, with the path echoing "Jai Mata Di" chants from thousands of devotees.

Vaishno Devi Yatra Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Katra

Early morning departure from Delhi via NH44. Drive through Ambala, Jalandhar, and Jammu to reach Katra by evening. The journey passes through Punjab and enters the beautiful foothills of Jammu. Check in to hotel in Katra, collect your yatra parchi (registration slip) from the Yatra Registration Counter, and rest for the trek ahead.

Distance: ~630 km from Delhi | 10-11 hrs drive
Day 2

Trek to Bhawan — Darshan & Return to Katra

Start the 12 km trek early morning (2-4 AM recommended). The well-paved path passes through Banganga, Adhkuwari (midpoint with another holy cave), and Hathi Matha before reaching Bhawan. Have darshan of the three holy Pindis inside the sacred cave. After darshan, trek back to Katra (or take helicopter/pony/palki). Rest in Katra.

Trek: 12 km uphill + 12 km return | Full day
Day 3

Return to Delhi

After breakfast, depart from Katra for Delhi. Optional stop at Jammu for Raghunath Temple or Bahu Fort. Arrive in Delhi by late evening. Alternatively, extend the trip to visit Patnitop hill station or Amritsar Golden Temple.

Distance: ~630 km | 10-11 hrs drive

Best Time to Visit Vaishno Devi

  • March - October: The best period for yatra. Pleasant weather, clear trekking path. Peak season with longer waiting times for darshan.
  • Navratri (April & October): The most auspicious time to visit. Massive rush of devotees — book everything well in advance.
  • November - February: Winter season — heavy snowfall may disrupt the trek. Path can be slippery. Carry heavy woolens if visiting in winter.

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable trekking shoes — the 12 km trek requires sturdy footwear
  • Warm clothing — temperatures drop significantly at Bhawan (5,200 ft altitude)
  • Raincoat/poncho — sudden rain is common in the mountains
  • Water bottle and light snacks — refreshment stalls are available but carry your own
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID) — mandatory for yatra registration
  • Walking stick — available for rent at Katra, highly recommended
  • Torch/flashlight — essential if starting the trek before dawn

Important Tips

  • Register for yatra parchi online at maavaishnodevi.org or at Katra counter — it is mandatory
  • Start the trek by 2-4 AM to reach Bhawan by morning and avoid afternoon heat
  • Helicopter service is available (Katra to Sanjichhat) — book in advance during peak season
  • Ponies, palki (palanquin), and battery cars are available for those who cannot trek
  • Lockers are available at Bhawan to store bags before entering the cave
  • Mobile network (Jio, Airtel) works at Katra and intermittently on the trek route
