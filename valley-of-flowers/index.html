About Valley of Flowers

Valley of Flowers is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most beautiful places on earth. Nestled in the high Himalayan region of Chamoli district, this 87.5 sq km valley bursts into a spectacular carpet of wildflowers every monsoon season, with over 600 species of wildflowers blooming from July to September.

The trek starts from Govindghat (after Joshimath) and covers 17 km to the valley via Ghangaria, the base camp. The valley is surrounded by towering Himalayan peaks, cascading waterfalls, and pristine glacial streams. You can combine this trek with a visit to Hemkund Sahib, the revered Sikh shrine located at 4,329m. The valley is open only from July to October — a short window to witness nature's most stunning floral display.

Valley of Flowers Itinerary (6 Days)

Day 1

Haridwar to Joshimath

Early morning pickup in our Tata Winger. Scenic drive through Devprayag, Rudraprayag, Karnaprayag, and Chamoli to Joshimath. Enjoy the beautiful confluence points along the way. Overnight stay at Joshimath.

Distance: ~270 km | Drive: 10-12 hrs
Day 2

Joshimath to Govindghat, Trek to Ghangaria

Short drive to Govindghat in the Tata Winger. Begin the 14 km trek to Ghangaria, the base camp for both Valley of Flowers and Hemkund Sahib. Trek along the Lakshman Ganga river through beautiful forests. Overnight stay at Ghangaria.

Drive: 25 km | Trek: 14 km | Time: 5-7 hrs trekking
Day 3

Valley of Flowers Day Trek

Trek from Ghangaria to Valley of Flowers (4 km one way). Spend the day surrounded by hundreds of species of wildflowers — brahmakamal, blue poppy, cobra lily, marigold, orchids, and many more. Photography paradise. Return to Ghangaria by evening.

Trek: 8 km round trip | Time: 5-6 hrs
Day 4

Hemkund Sahib Day Trek

Trek from Ghangaria to Hemkund Sahib (6 km one way), the revered Sikh shrine at 4,329m. The glacial lake surrounded by seven peaks is a sight to behold. Visit the Gurudwara and Lakshman Temple. Return to Ghangaria.

Trek: 12 km round trip | Time: 6-8 hrs
Day 5

Trek Back to Govindghat

Trek 14 km downhill from Ghangaria to Govindghat. The descent is easier and takes about 4-5 hours. Our Tata Winger will be waiting. Drive to Joshimath or Pipalkoti for overnight stay.

Trek: 14 km down | Time: 4-5 hrs
Day 6

Return to Haridwar

Comfortable drive back in the Tata Winger through the scenic Alaknanda valley. Optional stops at confluence points. Drop at Haridwar/Rishikesh by evening.

Distance: ~270 km | Drive: 9-10 hrs

Best Time to Visit Valley of Flowers

The valley is open only from July to October. The best months to visit are:

  • July - August: Peak blooming season. Maximum variety of flowers. Expect rain — carry rain gear.
  • September: Late blooming flowers, fewer crowds, clearer weather. Brahmakamal blooms in late August-September.

The valley remains closed from November to June due to heavy snowfall. Plan your trip only during the open season.

Things to Carry

  • Good quality rain jacket and rain cover for backpack (mandatory — it rains daily)
  • Waterproof trekking shoes with excellent grip
  • Warm layers — fleece, thermal innerwear, woolen cap, gloves
  • Trekking poles (highly recommended for the 14 km trek)
  • Water bottle, energy bars, dry fruits
  • Personal medicines, ORS packets (for altitude)
  • Camera with waterproof cover
  • Valid ID proof (required for entry)

Important Tips

  • Entry permit is required — available at Ghangaria forest office
  • Do not pluck any flowers — it's a protected UNESCO site with strict rules
  • Acclimatize at Joshimath before starting the trek to avoid altitude sickness
  • Ponies and porters available at Govindghat for those who need assistance
  • Accommodation at Ghangaria is basic — book in advance during peak season
  • No mobile network at Ghangaria or in the valley — inform family beforehand
  • Carry cash — no ATMs available beyond Joshimath
