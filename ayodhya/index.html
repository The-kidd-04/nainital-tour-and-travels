About Ayodhya

Ayodhya, the ancient city on the banks of the sacred Saryu River, is one of the seven holiest cities in Hinduism. It is revered as the birthplace of Lord Ram and has been a center of faith and devotion for thousands of years.

The grand Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Mandir, inaugurated in January 2024, is now one of the most visited temples in India. Built in the Nagara style of architecture, the temple complex is a marvel of Indian craftsmanship. Besides Ram Mandir, Ayodhya is home to Hanuman Garhi, Kanak Bhawan, Dashrath Mahal, and the beautiful Saryu River ghats.

Ayodhya Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Ayodhya

Early morning departure from Delhi in our Tata Winger. Drive via Lucknow Expressway. Arrive in Ayodhya by evening. Visit Saryu Ghat for evening aarti. Overnight stay at Ayodhya.

Distance: ~630 km | Drive: 10-11 hrs
Day 2

Ayodhya Darshan — Full Day

Early morning visit to Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Mandir. Then visit Hanuman Garhi, Kanak Bhawan, Dashrath Mahal, Nageshwarnath Temple, and Tulsi Smarak Bhawan. Evening Saryu Aarti at Ram Ki Paidi ghats. Overnight stay.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Return to Delhi (or Prayagraj/Varanasi extension)

Morning darshan and Saryu snan. Drive back to Delhi. Optional extension to Prayagraj Sangam (170 km) or Varanasi (200 km). Reach Delhi by late evening.

Distance: ~630 km | Drive: 10-11 hrs

Best Time to Visit Ayodhya

  • October - March: Best time — pleasant weather (10°C - 25°C). Comfortable for temple visits.
  • Ram Navami (March/April): Grand celebrations at the temple. Massive crowds but an unforgettable experience.
  • Diwali (Oct/Nov): Ayodhya's Deepotsav is world-famous — lakhs of diyas lit on Saryu ghats.
  • Parikrama Day: Special 14 km parikrama of Ayodhya — very auspicious.

Avoid April-June — intense heat in UP plains (40°C+).

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable clothes suitable for temple visit
  • Comfortable walking shoes (lots of walking between temples)
  • Sunscreen and hat (if visiting in warmer months)
  • Cash — for temple donations and local shopping
  • Valid ID proof (mandatory for Ram Mandir entry)
  • Mobile phones may not be allowed inside Ram Mandir — check latest rules

Important Tips

  • Ram Mandir darshan requires online/offline pass — check latest process before visiting
  • Reach Ram Mandir early morning (5 AM) to avoid long queues
  • Bags, cameras, and electronics may not be allowed inside — use locker facility
  • Visit Hanuman Garhi first (it's tradition to seek Hanuman ji's blessings before Ram Mandir)
  • Don't miss the evening Saryu Aarti at Ram Ki Paidi — truly mesmerizing
  • Try local Ayodhya pedha and thali at restaurants near Hanuman Garhi
