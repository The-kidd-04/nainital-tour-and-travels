About Kanatal

Kanatal is a small, peaceful village situated between Mussoorie and Chamba on the Mussoorie-Chamba road. At an altitude of 2,590 meters, it offers cool mountain air, apple orchards, and dense forests that remain untouched by commercial tourism.

The Surkanda Devi Temple, a short trek from the road, rewards you with panoramic 360-degree views of the Himalayan ranges. Kodia Jungle is perfect for nature walks and birdwatching. Camp Kanatal offers adventure activities like rappelling, zip lining, and rock climbing for thrill-seekers. The massive Tehri Dam is nearby and makes for an impressive side trip. Kanatal is one of those rare places that hasn't been commercialized yet — visit before it changes.

Kanatal Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Drive from Dehradun/Mussoorie, Surkanda Devi Trek & Camping

Morning drive to Kanatal from Dehradun or Mussoorie. After settling in, trek to Surkanda Devi Temple for panoramic Himalayan views. Evening at camp — enjoy bonfire, stargazing, and adventure activities like rappelling and zip line.

Dehradun: ~78 km | Mussoorie: ~45 km
Day 2

Kodia Jungle, Tehri Dam & Return

Morning nature walk through Kodia Jungle — dense forest with beautiful birdlife. Drive to Tehri Dam to witness one of Asia's tallest dams. Drive back to Dehradun/Mussoorie by evening.

Tehri Dam: ~40 km from Kanatal

Best Time to Visit Kanatal

Kanatal offers something special in every season:

  • March - June: Pleasant weather, ideal for trekking and camping. Temperature: 10°C to 22°C.
  • September - November: Clear skies, excellent views, comfortable temperatures.
  • December - February: Snowfall turns Kanatal into a winter wonderland. Temperature: -2°C to 8°C.

Monsoon (July-August) brings heavy rain — roads can be slippery. Best avoided.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — Kanatal is cold even in summer due to high altitude
  • Comfortable trekking shoes
  • Rain jacket or poncho
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Water bottle, dry snacks
  • Valid ID proof
  • Cash — limited ATM availability

Important Tips

  • Surkanda Devi trek is short (2 km) but steep — wear proper shoes
  • Adventure camp activities should be booked in advance during peak season
  • Carry warm layers even in summer — temperature drops significantly at night
  • Mobile network is limited — BSNL works in some areas
  • Combine with Dhanaulti (nearby) for a more complete trip
  • Tehri Dam offers water sports — check availability on arrival
