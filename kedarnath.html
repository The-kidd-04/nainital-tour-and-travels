About Kedarnath

Kedarnath is one of the holiest Hindu pilgrimage sites in India, nestled in the mighty Garhwal Himalayan range at an altitude of 3,583 meters. The Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and part of the sacred Char Dham Yatra circuit.

The temple is believed to be over 1,000 years old, built by Adi Shankaracharya. Surrounded by breathtaking snow-capped peaks including Kedarnath peak (6,940m), the temple stands as a testament to ancient Indian architecture and devotion. The Mandakini river flows nearby, adding to the serene and spiritual atmosphere.

Kedarnath Trip Itinerary (3-4 Days)

Day 1

Haridwar / Rishikesh to Guptkashi

Early morning pickup in our Tata Winger. Drive through scenic route via Devprayag, Rudraprayag to Guptkashi (approx 8-9 hours). Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple. Overnight stay at Guptkashi.

Distance: ~210 km | Drive: 8-9 hrs
Day 2

Guptkashi to Kedarnath

Early drive to Gaurikund (30 km). Begin the 16 km trek to Kedarnath Temple (or take pony/palki/helicopter). Reach Kedarnath by afternoon. Evening darshan at the temple. Overnight stay near temple.

Trek: 16 km | Time: 6-8 hrs trekking
Day 3

Kedarnath to Guptkashi / Rudraprayag

Early morning darshan. Trek back down to Gaurikund (4-5 hours). Our Tata Winger will be waiting. Drive back to Guptkashi or Rudraprayag. Overnight stay.

Trek: 16 km down | Drive: 1 hr
Day 4

Return to Haridwar / Rishikesh

Comfortable drive back in the Tata Winger. Optional stop at Devprayag (Sangam of Alaknanda & Bhagirathi rivers). Drop at Haridwar/Rishikesh by evening.

Distance: ~210 km | Drive: 7-8 hrs

Best Time to Visit Kedarnath

Kedarnath Temple opens in late April/early May (on Akshaya Tritiya) and closes in November (on Bhai Dooj). The best months to visit are:

  • May - June: Pleasant weather, clear skies, ideal for trekking. Temperature: 5°C to 15°C.
  • September - October: Post-monsoon, lush green valleys, fewer crowds. Temperature: 3°C to 12°C.

Avoid July-August due to heavy monsoon rains and landslide risks on the roads.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — jacket, thermals, gloves, woolen cap (even in summer)
  • Comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Rain poncho / umbrella
  • Personal medicines, first aid
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Power bank for phone

Important Tips

  • Register for the yatra at Sonprayag (mandatory biometric registration)
  • Start the trek early (5-6 AM) to avoid afternoon weather changes
  • Helicopter booking can be done via IRCTC or private operators
  • Ponies/Palki available at Gaurikund for those who can't trek
  • ATMs are not available at Kedarnath — carry enough cash
  • Mobile network is limited — BSNL works in some areas
