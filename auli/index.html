About Auli

Auli — the skiing paradise of India. Surrounded by some of the mightiest Himalayan peaks including Nanda Devi (India's 2nd highest peak), Kamet, and Mana Parvat, Auli offers a visual spectacle that few places in India can match. It is home to Asia's longest gondola ride (4 km) connecting Joshimath to Auli.

In winter (December-February), Auli transforms into a snow wonderland — skiing, snowboarding, and snow trekking become the main attractions. In summer, lush green meadows stretch across the slopes with panoramic Himalayan views in every direction. Auli was historically used as a training ground for the Indian Army's winter warfare division. It also serves as the gateway to the famous Valley of Flowers and Hemkund Sahib.

Best Time to Visit Auli

Auli offers two distinct experiences depending on the season:

  • December - February: Peak snow season. Ideal for skiing, snowboarding, and snow trekking. Auli is blanketed in white snow. Temperature: -5°C to 5°C.
  • March: Snow starts melting, creating a beautiful mix of white and green. Pleasant weather for sightseeing. Temperature: 0°C to 10°C.
  • June - October: Lush green meadows, clear Himalayan views, perfect for trekking and nature walks. Temperature: 5°C to 15°C.

Avoid April-May (transition period) and November (early winter with unpredictable weather).

Things to Carry

  • Heavy winter clothes — down jacket, thermals, multiple warm layers
  • Snow boots with good grip and waterproofing
  • Gloves, woolen cap, muffler, thermal wear
  • Sunglasses (essential — snow glare can be intense)
  • Sunscreen (high SPF — UV reflection off snow is strong)
  • Medicines for altitude sickness, personal first aid
  • Water bottle, dry snacks, energy bars
  • Power bank for phone
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
