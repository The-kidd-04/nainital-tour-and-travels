About Mathura & Vrindavan

Mathura and Vrindavan, the sacred twin cities of Uttar Pradesh, are the birthplace and childhood home of Lord Krishna. Situated on the banks of the holy Yamuna River, these cities attract millions of devotees and tourists every year who come seeking divine blessings and spiritual peace.

From the revered Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple in Mathura to the enchanting Banke Bihari Temple and the stunning Prem Mandir in Vrindavan, every corner of Braj Bhoomi resonates with Krishna's leelas. The world-famous Holi celebrations in Barsana and Vrindavan, the melodious kirtans at ISKCON Temple, and the mesmerizing Yamuna Aarti make this a journey of a lifetime for every devotee.

Mathura & Vrindavan Trip Itinerary (2-3 Days)

Day 1

Delhi to Mathura — Janmabhoomi & Yamuna Aarti

Early morning departure from Delhi. Arrive in Mathura by afternoon. Visit Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, Dwarkadhish Temple, and Vishram Ghat. Witness the beautiful Yamuna Aarti at sunset — a truly divine experience. Overnight stay in Mathura or Vrindavan.

Distance: ~180 km from Delhi | 3-4 hrs drive
Day 2

Vrindavan Full Day — Banke Bihari, Prem Mandir & ISKCON

Full day exploring Vrindavan. Morning darshan at the famous Banke Bihari Temple (arrive early to avoid crowds). Visit Prem Mandir — a magnificent white marble temple with stunning light show in the evening. Explore ISKCON Temple (Sri Krishna Balaram Mandir), Radha Raman Temple, and Nidhivan. Evening kirtan at ISKCON.

Vrindavan local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Barsana / Gokul & Return

Morning visit to Barsana — the birthplace of Radha Rani, famous for Lathmar Holi. Visit Radha Rani Temple on the hilltop. Optional visit to Gokul — where Lord Krishna spent his childhood, and Govardhan for parikrama. Depart for Delhi by afternoon.

Barsana: 50 km from Mathura | Return to Delhi

Best Time to Visit Mathura & Vrindavan

  • October - March: Winter season — pleasant weather (10°C - 25°C). Best time for temple darshan and sightseeing.
  • Holi (March): The most iconic time to visit! Lathmar Holi in Barsana and Phoolon ki Holi in Banke Bihari Temple are world-famous celebrations.
  • Janmashtami (August - September): Krishna's birthday — grand celebrations across all temples. Midnight darshan at Janmabhoomi is unforgettable.

Things to Carry

  • Modest clothing — these are sacred temple towns, dress respectfully
  • Comfortable walking shoes (lots of temple walking!)
  • Socks — required for entering most temples (shoes not allowed inside)
  • Water bottle — stay hydrated, especially in summer months
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Cash — many local shops and donation counters prefer cash
  • Carry a bag for prasad and temple offerings

Important Tips

  • Visit Banke Bihari Temple early morning to avoid heavy crowds, especially on weekends
  • Photography is NOT allowed inside Banke Bihari Temple — keep your phone safely stored
  • Prem Mandir light and fountain show starts after sunset — don't miss it
  • Beware of monkeys in Vrindavan — keep food items in bags and secure your belongings
  • Try local delicacies — Mathura ke pede, lassi, and kachori are must-try treats
  • During Holi season, book accommodation well in advance as the city gets extremely crowded
