About Shimla & Manali

Shimla and Manali are two of India's most beloved hill stations, nestled in the heart of Himachal Pradesh. Shimla, the former summer capital of British India, enchants visitors with its colonial architecture, The Ridge, Mall Road, and panoramic Himalayan views. Manali, set in the Kullu Valley along the Beas River, is a paradise for adventure lovers and honeymooners alike.

From the toy-train charm of Shimla to the snow-capped grandeur of Rohtang Pass, from paragliding at Solang Valley to the hippie vibes of Old Manali — this combo trip offers the perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and natural beauty. The journey itself through lush green valleys and winding mountain roads is an experience you will never forget.

Shimla & Manali Trip Itinerary (5-6 Days)

Day 1

Delhi / Chandigarh to Shimla

Early morning departure in our Tata Winger. Drive via Chandigarh and the scenic Kalka-Shimla highway. Arrive Shimla by evening. Walk on Mall Road and enjoy the mountain breeze. Overnight at Shimla.

Distance: ~350 km (from Delhi) | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 2

Shimla Local Sightseeing

Visit The Ridge, Christ Church, Jakhoo Temple (Hanuman statue), Kufri for horse riding and snow activities. Evening shopping at Mall Road and Lakkar Bazaar.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 3

Shimla to Manali

After breakfast, drive from Shimla to Manali via Kullu. Stop at Kullu Shawl factories and the beautiful Beas River valley. Arrive Manali by evening. Explore Old Manali and cafes.

Distance: ~260 km | Drive: 7-8 hrs
Day 4

Manali — Solang Valley & Rohtang Pass

Drive to Solang Valley for paragliding, zorbing, and snow activities. Continue to Rohtang Pass (3,978m) for breathtaking glacier views (permit required, seasonal). Return to Manali.

Distance: ~50 km | Drive: 2-3 hrs (one way)
Day 5

Manali Local Sightseeing

Visit Hadimba Temple, Manu Temple, Vashisht Hot Springs, Tibetan Monastery, and Club House. Afternoon free for Mall Road shopping. Evening bonfire at hotel.

Local sightseeing | Full day
Day 6

Return Journey — Manali to Delhi

Early morning departure from Manali. Drive back via Kullu, Mandi, and Chandigarh. Arrive Delhi by late evening. Alternatively, split into 2 days with overnight halt at Chandigarh.

Distance: ~530 km | Drive: 10-12 hrs

Best Time to Visit Shimla & Manali

  • March - June: Pleasant summer weather (10°C - 25°C). Best for sightseeing, Rohtang Pass opens in May-June. Peak tourist season.
  • July - September: Monsoon season — lush green valleys but landslide risk on highways. Avoid if possible.
  • October - November: Post-monsoon clarity. Beautiful autumn colors, fewer crowds, comfortable weather.
  • December - February: Snowfall season. Kufri and Solang Valley covered in snow. Magical experience but roads can be tricky.

Things to Carry

  • Warm clothes — even in summer, mornings and evenings are cold at altitude
  • Comfortable trekking/walking shoes with good grip
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses (high UV at altitude)
  • Rain jacket / umbrella (especially during monsoon)
  • Valid ID proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID)
  • Motion sickness medicine — the roads are very winding
  • Camera and power bank — you will need them constantly!

Important Tips

  • Book Rohtang Pass permits online in advance — limited daily quota
  • Carry warm clothes even in summer — Rohtang Pass can be freezing
  • Negotiate adventure activity prices at Solang Valley before starting
  • Try local Himachali dishes — Siddu, Dham, Tudkiya Bhath
  • Buy Kullu shawls and Shimla apples directly from local shops
  • Jio and Airtel work in Shimla and Manali town; signal drops in remote areas
