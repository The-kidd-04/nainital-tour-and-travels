About Binsar

Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary is a hidden gem in the Kumaon Himalayas, home to over 200 bird species, leopards, Himalayan black bear, and a rich diversity of flora and fauna. Spread across dense forests of oak, rhododendron, and pine, it is one of the most peaceful destinations in Uttarakhand with minimal tourist crowds.

The highlight of Binsar is Zero Point, which offers a breathtaking 300km panoramic view of the mighty Himalayan range including Kedarnath, Chaukhamba, Trishul, Nanda Devi, and Panchachuli peaks. The ancient Binsar Mahadev Temple, surrounded by thick forest, adds a spiritual touch to this nature retreat. Binsar is perfect for nature walks, birdwatching, and photography.

Binsar Trip Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1

Drive to Binsar, Nature Walk & Sunset at Zero Point

Morning pickup from Almora or Nainital. Scenic drive through oak and pine forests to Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary. Check in and enjoy a leisurely nature walk through the sanctuary trails. Head to Zero Point for a spectacular sunset with panoramic Himalayan views.

From Almora: ~30 km | From Nainital: ~95 km
Day 2

Early Morning Birding, Binsar Mahadev Temple & Return

Wake up early for birdwatching in the sanctuary — spot Himalayan monals, woodpeckers, and many more species. Visit the ancient Binsar Mahadev Temple set amidst dense forest. After lunch, begin the return drive to Almora or Nainital.

Birding: 2-3 hrs | Temple visit: 1 hr

Best Time to Visit Binsar

Binsar is beautiful year-round, but the best months to visit are:

  • April - June: Pleasant weather, clear Himalayan views, wildflowers in bloom. Temperature: 10°C to 25°C.
  • September - November: Post-monsoon clarity, excellent birdwatching, crisp mountain air. Temperature: 5°C to 20°C.

Winter (December-February) brings snowfall — beautiful but cold. Monsoon (July-August) has leeches on trails but lush greenery.

Things to Carry

  • Layered clothing — light jacket, fleece, windbreaker
  • Comfortable walking/trekking shoes
  • Binoculars for birdwatching
  • Camera with zoom lens for wildlife photography
  • Water bottle, snacks, energy bars
  • Insect repellent (especially during monsoon)
  • Valid ID proof (required at sanctuary entry)

Important Tips

  • Entry permit is required for the wildlife sanctuary — available at the gate
  • Reach Zero Point before sunset for the best Himalayan views
  • Maintain silence on trails — you'll spot more birds and wildlife
  • No restaurants inside the sanctuary — carry packed food or eat at your stay
  • Mobile network is limited — BSNL works in some areas
  • Hire a local guide for birdwatching — they know the best spots
